Haoyu Zhang is a Ph.D. student in Computer Science at Princeton University
(expected to graduate in 2018). During his Ph.D. study, he worked on designing
and building scalable and highly efficient distributed systems for big-data
analytics and machine learning, fault-tolerant network architecture, and
strongly consistent and ultrafast key-value service with programmable networks
(P4). He has published in several systems conferences including NSDI, SOSP and
SOCC. He previously interned at Facebook and Microsoft Research, working on
optimized Spark shuffle service and video analytics systems, both deployed and
running in production.
